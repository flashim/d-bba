Welcome to Didsbury Black Belt Academy

Rules of the School
Arrival Times
  • Please ensure that you arrive and are ready to start the class on time
  • Please take care not to disturb classes when you arrive or leave the school
Courtesy & Respect
  • Bow when you enter and leave the school
  • Bow before you step on and off the training area.
  • Bow before commencing partner work
  • Be respectful to all members, instructors and visitors whilst in the school.
  • Address instructors as either ‘Sir’ or ‘Miss’ or as Mr, Mrs, or Miss ‘Smith’.
Dress Code
  • Full uniform must be worn in class: white suit or black/red/white tee-shirt & black trousers
  • Belts should be worn and tied securely.
  • ‘T’ shirts must be tucked in to trousers.
  • Uniforms should be kept clean, pressed and in a good state of repair.
  • Badges must be worn: on trousers, on the left leg 6” down from the waist On Jackets, positioned on the left arm 3” from the shoulder.
  • Failure to wear the full uniform may result in you being unable to train in class.
Personal Safety
  • Finger and toenails must be kept clean & short at all times.
  • Outdoor shoes are not permitted on the training area
  • Martial arts training shoes are acceptable providing they are not also worn outdoors .
  • Wearing socks without shoes is dangerous and is NOT permitted under any circumstances.
  • It is the members responsibility to maintain insurance cover. (Insurance is £25 a year)
  • Take care of your own safety and that of those around you.
  • Members training in a dangerous manner will be removed from the training area.
Safety Equipment
  • Only safety equipment provided by the school is allowed in class.
  • Training bags should be kept away from the matted area or in places where people can trip over them.
  • Full Protective equipment should be worn during all partner work.
Spectators
  • Spectators are welcome and expected to observe the same mat etiquette as members.
  • Do not shout to members while they are training.
  • Please make sure conversations remain at low volume so as not disturb classes.

The 12 Characteristics of Black Belt Parents
Black Belt Parents Always:
  • See their children safely in and out of class
  • Watch their children in class whenever possible
  • Don’t shout out to correct their children in class
  • Are always quiet during the class
  • Support the school message of courtesy and respect for others
  • Keep their children’s uniform clean and tidy
  • Bow into and out of the class with their children
  • Understand that children develop at different speeds
  • Never criticize their child’s ability instead they offer praise and encouragement
  • Understand that time will cure most ‘technical’ problems
  • Set the example of who they want their child to become
  • Understand that sometimes their child will not want to come to class and that this is normal but persisting through these times will bring great benefits
